*In the foothills of the Sahyadri mountains, where the mist speaks and trees remember, a sacred forest has stood since before time had a name. The Warli people painted its spirits on every mud wall of their village — white chalk figures dancing in endless circles, a record of all that was holy.*

*But one harvest season, the paintings began to fade.*
*The sacred circles broke. The spirit figures vanished from the walls.*
*And in the forest — silence, where song had always lived.*

<br/>

*Aru was neither the oldest nor the strongest of the village. But when the elder spoke, her eyes found Aru's.*

*The trees here were older than memory. Their trunks bore the white scratch-marks of ancient Warli prayers. Spirits drifted between the branches — not hostile, but lost.*

*Aru collected the sun symbols and the grain offerings, pressing each one to the bark of the oldest trees. One by one, a spirit paused. Remembered. Rose.*

**Warli: Spirit of the Forest** is a side-scrolling runner adventure. Aru runs automatically through the forest — your role is to guide her, collect the sacred symbols, and keep her safe from the encroaching spirits.

<br/>

<div align="center">

| ☀ Action | ⬡ Control | ◎ Effect |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Jump** | `SPACE` or `↑` or `TAP` | Leap over obstacles |
| **Collect Symbols** | Run into collectibles | Earn sacred points |
| **Avoid Obstacles** | Jump over stones & spirits | Keep your 3 lives |
| **Complete Level** | Survive 60 seconds | Advance the story |

</div>

<br/>

```
  ☀  Sun Symbol    ──  10 points  ──  Light restored to the forest
  ⊛  Grain Symbol  ──   5 points  ──  Harvest blessed by the spirits  
  ◉  Spirit Orb    ──  20 points  ──  A lost spirit finds its way home
```

**Warli art** is one of the oldest surviving folk art traditions in India, practiced by the **Warli tribe** of the Sahyadri mountain range in Maharashtra. Formally documented in the early 1970s, the tradition itself stretches back millennia.

### The Visual Language

```
  ▲  Triangles    ── human bodies, mountains, trees
  ●  Circles      ── heads, the sun, the moon, the world
  —  Lines        ── arms, legs, connection, movement
  ⊡  Squares      ── the Chauk — sacred ritual space
```

Painted in **white rice paste on mud walls**, Warli art depicts daily life — farming, dancing, hunting, wedding ceremonies — and the spiritual world that lives alongside it.

> *No claim of ownership over the Warli art form is made.*
> *The tradition belongs to the community that created and sustains it.*
